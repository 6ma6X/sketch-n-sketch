Sketch-N-Sketch [Tutorial]

Introduction

Welcome to the Sketch-n-Sketch user study, and thanks for participating!

This tutorial is designed to teach you the basics of how to use Sketch-n-Sketch. It is divided into five separate lessons, each designed to cover a few key topics. Each lesson discusses several examples, designed to be worked in Sketch-n-Sketch v0.3 while you are reading. We have also sprinkled in a set of exercises in each lesson that we hope will offer instructive and fun opportunities to get your hands dirty. Very roughly speaking, we expect each lesson to take approximately one to three hours, with the last two (optional) lessons covering somewhat more advanced material. But, your mileage may vary depend on your background and interests... and how much pleasure or pain you're experiencing with the tool!

To help us understand what aspects of Sketch-n-Sketch and this tutorial work well and what aspects need improvement, we have placed brief survey questions at the end of each lesson, as well as at the end of the entire sequence. Please fill out the surveys as you work through the tutorial, as opposed to all at once at the end, because we are interested to hear how your reactions and thoughts progress.

Please submit your surveys by Friday, September 18, 2015. We encourage you to fill out the Exit Survey even if you haven't finished all five lessons by that point — in this case, you may want to skim the remaining articles to get a flavor of what's covered. We will be very happy to hear your feedback after September 18, but we may not be able to incorporate your wonderful feedback into the summary of results from the study. (UPDATE 09/22/15: Please continue to submit surveys whenever you finish them.)

Thanks again for participating, we appreciate the time you are spending!

A Note About Anonymity

Rather than setting up a robust identification system, we have opted for a simple, self-reporting scheme based on an honor system: please pick a simple ID Token for yourself and enter it the same way in each of the surveys. This will allow us to correlate your responses throughout the tutorial. There is no registration process for ID tokens, so choose one as you see fit. You may decide to use your actual name as your token, or you may choose another moniker if you wish to remain anonymous. We ask only that you choose something that is likely to be unique. For example, "Bill" and "Sarah" are probably not good choices, but "BillAndSarahHeartSketchNSketchKThxBye" probably is — okay, well, maybe not anymore, but you get the idea.

Happy sketching and sketching, Bill, Sarah, or whoever you are!
